**How to use the Ready button in my app**

The Ready Button, also known as "Mark Order Ready," is a feature in your GrabMerchant app that allows you to notify delivery partners that an order is prepared for pickup. Using this feature ensures a smoother and faster order fulfillment process, improving customer satisfaction and streamlining your operations.

This guide will walk you through the steps to use the Ready Button effectively and explain its broader impact on your performance metrics and operational accuracy.

**Step-by-Step Instructions**

* Open the GrabMerchant App  
* Access Your Orders  
* Locate the specific order that is ready for pickup  
* Mark the order as ‘Ready’

  # **Why Accurate Usage of MOR Matters**

  **Impact on Performance Metrics**  
  Using the Ready Button responsibly is critical to maintaining your performance metrics. Prematurely marking an order as ready when it is not fully prepared can lead to:  
* Higher Order Delay Rate (ODR): This metric reflects the percentage of orders delayed due to incorrect statuses or long delivery-partner wait time at your store. MOR abuse can increase your ODR, negatively impacting your Merchant Quality Program (MQP) score.  
* Lower Merchant Quality Program (MQP) Score: MQP is a key indicator of your overall service quality on the Grab platform. Inaccurate usage of MOR can result in a reduced MQP score due to the ODR metric, affecting your ranking and visibility on the platform. [Find out more about MQP here](https://help.grab.com/merchant/en-sg/900007229123#).  
  **Improving Estimated Prep Time Accuracy**  
  When MOR is used correctly, it helps our system learn and adjust the estimated preparation time for your future orders. This ensures:  
* Better alignment between actual prep times and delivery-partner arrival times.  
* Improved operational efficiency and reduced waiting times for delivery-partners.  
* Enhanced customer satisfaction due to faster and more accurate order fulfillment.  
  **Best Practices**  
* Timeliness: Ensure you only mark an order as ready when it is completely prepared and packaged for pickup.  
* Accuracy: Double-check the order details before marking it ready to avoid any errors such as missing or wrong items.  
* Communication: If there are any delays, use the app's communication tools to inform our delivery-partners.  
* For more best practices, you may refer to [this article](https://help.grab.com/merchant/en-sg/900007229123#).

**General programme overview**

Top Service was previously known as Quality Hero Programme.

**\> What has changed from the previous Quality Hero programme to the new Top Service programme?**   
Major changes include:

- Tiered system: Instead of just "Quality" or "Non-Quality," there are now 4 tiers (Tier 1, 2, 3\)  
- More rewards: Exceptional service now gets extra visibility and benefits  
- Consequences for poor quality: Tier 4 merchants face restrictions on certain programmes

**Common Questions on Improving your Tier**  
**1\. I am having difficulty reducing my Store Offline Rate and Order Delay Rate to meet the Top Service criteria.**

1. **Avoid Pausing your store too often. Instead, turn on Busy Mode.**  
   1. When your store gets busy, pausing can help you catch up with the influx of orders. However, this also raises your Offline Rate – the time your store was paused compared to your overall operating hours.   
   2. Use Busy Mode to slow new orders so you have the time to clear backlog and avoid delays\! This reduces both ODR and Offline Rate.  
   3. You can turn on Busy Mode on the Merchant App by navigating to the Orders tab. On the top right of Orders page, tap on the current mode (Normal/ Busy/ Paused) to change your store status.  
   4. Once selecting 'Busy' you can adjust the preparation time required for every new order that comes in by toggling the '+' or '-' buttons.

![][image27]![][image28]

2. **Edit your Order Preparation Time (OPT) within 3 mins of receiving your order.**  
   1. If you require more time to prepare an order, promptly edit your OPT upon receiving your order so that the order is not marked delayed – consider the size of the order and how busy your store is and set the time accordingly.   
   2. This will allow you more time to prepare orders when you’re busy, decreasing delayed orders and offline rate because you can avoid pausing your store.  
   3. You can Edit the OPT of your order by clicking on ‘View order’ and toggling the '+' or '-' buttons to set the time you require to prepare all subsequent incoming orders.  
   4. By setting OPT accurately a few times, you train the system to auto‑estimate OPT by order size. As its estimates improve, you’ll need fewer manual adjustments.

**2\. How is Order Delay Rate calculated?**  
The Order Delay Rate tracks how many of your fulfilled orders are completed on time by total number of orders.

An order is considered on time if:

* It is prepared within the specified prep time.  
* It is handed over to the driver within 3 minutes of their arrival at your store.

If you're experiencing a busy period, you can [extend the prep time](https://help.grab.com/merchant/en-sg/40000756-How-to-edit-your-Order-Preparation-Time-\(OPT\)) within the first 3 minutes after receiving the order. This allows you more flexibility to prepare the order on time. 

For more information on Order Delay Rate, please refer to [this article](https://help.grab.com/merchant/en-sg/40001076-Understanding-Order-Delay-Feature-and-Order-Delay-Rate#).

**3\. Why is my order marked as delayed even though I have marked the order as ready?**  
Here’s how the timing works to avoid delays:

* Scenario 1: After preparing the order, hand it over to the driver within 3 minutes of their arrival at your store. If the driver collects the order within this 3-minute window, the order is marked on time.  
* Scenario 2: If the driver arrives before the order is ready, prepare the order within the indicated prep time. Then, hand it over to the driver within 3 minutes of that prep time. If the order is handed over after 3 minutes past the indicated prep time, the order is marked delayed.  
* Scenario 3: If the driver arrives late, after the order is already prepped, hand over the order within 3 minutes of their arrival. If it takes longer than 3 minutes to pass the order to the driver, it is marked delayed.  
* Scenario 4: Sometimes, drivers might accidentally or inaccurately indicate that they arrived at the store and picked up the order, which may affect whether or not the order is marked delayed. In such situations, not to worry\! Grab uses GPS signals from drivers and merchants to verify their location at arrival and pick up to ensure that an order is accurately marked delayed.

To ensure your orders are not marked as delayed, always prepare the order as soon as it arrives and pass it to the driver promptly.

**5\. How is our current Order Prep Time (OPT) set?**  
Order Preparation Time (OPT) is determined using historical data and machine learning models.

* Machine learning models are used to predict OPT based on historical data (i.e., past order preparation times), cuisine types, order sizes and time of day.   
* Using the Mark Order Ready (MOR) button accurately improves the system's estimation of your OPT.  
* For newly onboarded merchants, OPT is estimated based on merchant details and menu types.

**Title:** What is the Beverage Container Return Scheme (BCRS)? 

From 1 April 2026, Singapore will implement the Beverage Container Return Scheme (BCRS), a nationwide initiative to encourage recycling of beverage containers.

Under this scheme, consumers will pay a **S$0.10 refundable deposit** for each [**eligible pre-packaged beverage**](https://www.bcrs.sg/retailers) purchased (for example, drinks in plastic or metal containers with the BCRS label). Consumers will receive this deposit back when they return the empty container at designated return points across Singapore. 

Beverage producers and importers are required to ensure all eligible containers carry the **mandatory BCRS label** from **1 October 2026**. The authorities have provided a transition period from **1 April to 30 September 2026** for merchants to clear existing non-BCRS labelled stock.

**What does this mean for your Grab store?**  
Grab is currently enhancing our systems so your Grab store can correctly handle and report the S$0.10 deposit in line with **BCRS** requirements. 

We will keep you updated on rollout timelines and any relevant regulatory developments as they emerge.

**Frequently Asked Questions:**

**➤**Basis of Scheme \<Tap to expand hidden content\>  
**1\.** **What is Grab’s role in the BCRS?**   
Grab is a platform where merchants sell food and beverage items, including eligible pre-packaged beverages under the BCRS scheme. Under the scheme, merchants are required to collect the S$0.10 deposit from consumers for each eligible item sold and pass it through to the scheme operator.

We are currently updating our platform to support the proper itemisation, collection and reporting of this deposit in line with NEA and IRAS requirements and will share more details as soon as possible

**2\.** **What happens if i do not collect the S$0.10 deposit from consumers for each eligible BCRS-item sold on my store**  
Merchants are required to charge the S$0.10 deposit for every eligible BCRS beverage sold. This deposit cannot be absorbed by the merchant and must be passed on to consumers in accordance with the scheme.

**3\.** **Is Grab or the merchant responsible for refunding the deposit to consumers?**  
No. The refund happens outside of the Grab platform: consumers get their S$0.10 back when they bring empty containers to designated BCRS return points (e.g. at supermarkets, community areas etc.) across Singapore, not via Grab or the merchant’s delivery order flow

**➤**Scope and Eligibility of Beverages \<Tap to expand hidden content\>  
**1\. Which beverages are covered under BCRS?**  
For more information with regards to eligible BCRS beverages, please refer to [https://bcrs.sg/faq](https://bcrs.sg/faq)

**2\. How will Grab determine which items are eligible under BCRS?**  
Eligibility will likely be determined through SKU-level tagging — using GTIN/barcode and/or a BCRS flag — so the system can automatically identify which items carry a deposit. We will share more on the implementation details closer to date. 

**3\. What about multi-packs (e.g. 6-packs, 24-can cartons)?**  
Under NEA rules, the deposit applies per container, not per bundle. For example:

* 6-pack → S$0.60 deposit

* 24-pack → S$2.40 deposit

We are working on ensuring bundle SKUs reflect this correctly in pricing and order breakdowns.

\<End of hidden content\>

**➤**Pricing, commission & GST \<Tap to expand hidden content\>  
**1\. How will the S$0.10 deposit appear in Merchant’s Grab menu price?**  
Each eligible beverage will have a separate S$0.10 “deposit” component added on top of the beverage price, and not bundled into the base item price in our systems. More details on the exact configuration will be shared closer to the product rollout date.

**2\.** **Will Grab charge commission on the S$0.10 beverage container deposit (BCRS)?**

For GrabFood & GrabMart delivery orders, the S$0.10 BCRS deposit will not be subject to commission. Such orders are itemised, which allows the deposit to be identified separately and excluded when calculating commission.

For Dine Out and GrabPay transactions, the payment reflects the total bill determined by the merchant at checkout, rather than individual item components processed by Grab. As a result, if a BCRS-eligible beverage is included in the transaction, the S$0.10 deposit will be included in the commission calculation.

**3\. Is GST charged on the S$0.10 deposit?**  
No, the deposit is not subjected to GST charges.

**4\. Is Grab Commission charged on the S$0.10 deposit?**   
No, the deposit will not be commissionable. The commission continues to apply only to the beverage price.

**5\. Can I just increase my drink prices by S$0.10 instead of itemising a deposit?**  
No. NEA requires that drinks with the BCRS deposit mark carry a distinct S$0.10 refundable deposit, not just a general price increase.
